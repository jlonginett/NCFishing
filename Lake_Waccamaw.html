Lake Waccamaw

Lake Waccamaw is a large, oval-shaped lake that has over 14 miles of shoreline. Limestone bluffs along the north shore act as filters for the water, making it ideal for a variety of aquatic life. Here you will find unique species of animals, rare plants, and endangered animals. Lake Waccamaw is the largest of the Carolina Bay Lakes with a surface area of nearly 9,000 acres. From its sandy shores to its tree-lined natural areas, Lake Waccamaw offers peaceful surroundings, an intriguing natural history, abundant recreational opportunities, and a vast array of available homes for sale. While this body of water is a natural wonder all on its own, it just may be the residential community surrounding the lake that is the most unique in nature. Probably one of the best white perch fishing lakes in North Carolina, Lake Waccamaw is under fished for virtually all fish species in the lake. This 8,900-acre lake, located in the south-east part of the state, beckons you to come enjoy the great fishing. It is a perfect lake for small craft - inflatable's, canoes, kayaks, john boats and aluminum boats fit the bill. As a result of plentiful vegetation and thriving perch and sunfish populations, bass and catfish thrive here and can grow to trophy size. With relatively clear water, most lures will attract bites. Smaller lures - crankbaits, spinners, and spinnerbaits, will attract bass, crappie, bluegill, white bass and perch. Using small sizes, you never know what will charge your lure next.
