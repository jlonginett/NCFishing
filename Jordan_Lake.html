Jordan Lake

Jordan Lake is a reservoir in New Hope Valley, west of Cary and south of Durham in Chatham County, North Carolina. It is part of the Jordan Lake State Recreation Area, the reservoir covers 13,940 acres (5,640 ha) with a shoreline of 180 miles (290 km) at its standard water level of 216 feet (66 m) above sea level. Bass and crappie dominate the attention of anglers at Jordan Lake. This is a 14,000-acre reservoir in the north-central part of the state, with a mixed variety of fish species to appeal to anyone who enjoys fresh-water fishing. Along with sunfish, striped bass, white bass and catfish round out the fishing options. Around 180 miles of shoreline provide plenty of fishing opportunities for kayak, boat, canoe or shoreline fishing. There is plenty of aquatic vegetation in the lake to keep it fertile and sustaining a healthy food chain. Marinas, campgrounds, eateries, lodging, stores and local guides are plentiful in the area. Boat rentals are available on the lake and there is public boat ramps for launching private watercraft. Come for a day of fishing or bring the family for an extended vacation here at Lake Jordan.
