Hyco Lake

Hyco Lake is a reservoir in Person and Caswell counties, North Carolina. It is the area's main destination for fishing, boating, water skiing, wake boarding, and recreation and is the larger of Person County's two lakes. The water is regulated by the North Carolina Department of Environment and Natural Resources. Duke Energy Progress owns the land surrounding the lake up to the 420-foot (130 m) above-sea-level mark. The lake covers 3,750 acres (1,520 ha), containing about 25 billion US gallons (95,000,000 m3) of water, with 120 miles (190 km) of shoreline. Year-round warm water, heated by the generating station, makes Hyco Lake a fish factory where bass and other species grow all year long. This 3,750-acre lake in the north-central part of the state, is primarily a bass and crappie fishery. There are however catfish and several species of sunfish, plus white bass to chase. This is the lake to fish when neighboring lakes turn cold for winter. The lake not only has nice quantities of bass, but quality as well...fish over 5 pounds are common and an occasional lunker exceeding 8 pounds. The marina has boats and kayaks for rent and there are places to camp around the lake. Some of the shoreline is accessible for fishing from shore.
