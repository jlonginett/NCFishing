Badin Lake (aka Badin Reservoir)

Badin is a 5,000 acre lake in the central part of the state, at New London, NC. With depths of over 180 feet, Badin Lake water stays cooler during summer than many of the other lakes in the area. As the shallows warm, the fish move deeper for comfort - following the bait fish. Bass, stripers, crappie, white bass and catfish are favored targets of local anglers. The sunfish populations are healthy and grow to good eating size. Over 100 miles of shoreline offers plenty of fishing opportunity - from shore or from a boat, canoe or kayak. Rocks, brush, boulders and boat docks are favorite targets for bass anglers. The lake has plenty of structure at varying depths as the fish move deeper. Reefs, ledges, points and humps, especially those with drop-offs into deeper creek channels, tend to hold fish as well as schools of baitfish. Campgrounds and RV parks are located around the lake, along with several boat ramps for convenient launching. Several marinas offer gas, boat rentals, fishing tackle, boating supplies, groceries and ice. There is also a grille with snacks, sandwiches and hot meals.
